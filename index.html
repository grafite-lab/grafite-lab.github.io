Grafite Lab

Focus on prototypes, not paperwork.

A micro grant fund run by students, for students.

In short, we are a community of students who want to merge multiple engineering disciplines and build things around them. We're student-funded. We pool part of our stipends and personal resources to help promising engineering projects get off the ground. The amounts aren't huge, but they're often enough to get a prototype built instead of waiting months for institutional funding.

Some of the things we've helped come to reality.

Engineering is not a collection of isolated branches. The modern obsession with fleeing to CS has blinded people to what real engineering is. The brutal yet beautiful friction of the physical world is what makes engineering fun in the first place! It's taking an idea out of your head and forcing it to exist in the real world. If it never leaves the screen, it's just a very confident PowerPoint (or a hackathon slop).

Making a hardware prototype is a lot of work, but it's often bottlenecked by a lack of funding and support. The college and clubs will fund you eventually (hopium), maybe after paperwork that takes months, for an amount that barely covers a breadboard. By the time approval comes through, the excitement is gone and so is the semester.

We exist to fix that specific problem. We're lean on purpose and have no hierarchy. We fund projects directly and fast, then handle the rest: the intros, the emails to people who'd never reply to a student, the connections to whoever's doing the wild stuff you're chasing (someone building satellites, someone doing computational biology, someone training world models to figure out where to dig a mine).

Our selection process is two conversations. That's it. We meet you in person, on campus, wherever you pick, and we talk. Once to hear what you're obsessed with, once to make sure you can build something even out of nothing.

The only currency here is trust, and we hand it over first, no strings attached. We're not going to test you before we help you. The only thing we ask in return is that you stay with the project through the part where it doesn't work and you don't know why. We can't make that part fun. We can make sure money and support aren't the reason you quit.
