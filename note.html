Note

We are shortcutting obscurity.

I got this idea while on a flight from Bengaluru to somewhere in North Bengal.

Oftentimes, the most interesting people get subdued by external factors, and the rigid system we have around us in India is one of the sole reasons for that. Too much bloat and hierarchy already exists. I get sick thinking about it. Permission this, permission that. Here's your syllabus, don't think more. Where's my roadmap? Please share the roadmap!

You are in 2026. Anything that can be framed as a loss function will be optimized. You are a human with flesh and bones, competing against mecha-monsters with trillions of tensor cores. You really think you would win in those cases?

Now, the handful of people who dare to think differently either stop or end up ruining their mental and physical health (like myself, earlier this year). Why? You start from obscurity with no one to back you up, neither in trust nor in money. I faced all of that firsthand. But by sheer luck and madness, I somehow pulled things off (tweet), and I desperately want others to not face that. It sucks. Sucks like a rotten egg mashed with spinach on a hot summer evening. That should make sense.

I now have the network to pull in people for pretty big projects, but I am no longer in need of more opportunities. For me, it's time to excel in what I already have. Hence, we back you up --- iff you have the clarity to do what you want to do (a pinch of autism sprinkled would be nice). Go build cool shit, and we will make sure you get to know the right people and get the right resources.

Rigidity is cringe. Free flow of free will ftw.

- Sagnik
